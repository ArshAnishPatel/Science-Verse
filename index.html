Class 6 Biology, Chemistry, Physics, Earth Science & Math

The Cell — Basic Unit of Life

Biology · Class 6

What is a Cell?

A cell is the smallest structural and functional unit of life. All living organisms are made of cells.

  • Cell Membrane — thin flexible layer controlling what enters/exits
  • Nucleus — control centre; contains DNA with genetic instructions
  • Cytoplasm — jelly-like fluid filling the cell
  • Mitochondria — powerhouse; produces energy (ATP)
  • Plant only: Cell Wall, Chloroplasts, Large Central Vacuole
  • Animal only: Centrioles, Lysosomes
🔑 Key FactPlant cells are rectangular; animal cells are irregular/circular in shape.
🌿

Food Chain & Energy Flow

Biology · Class 6

How Energy Flows

A food chain shows how energy passes from one organism to another through feeding relationships.

  • Producers — plants that make food via photosynthesis
  • Consumers — animals that eat plants or other animals
  • Decomposers — fungi & bacteria that break down dead matter
  • 10% Rule — only 10% of energy passes to the next level; 90% is lost as heat
Photosynthesis: 6CO₂ + 6H₂O + light → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂
⚗️

States of Matter

Chemistry · Class 6

The Three States of Matter

Matter exists in three main states depending on temperature and pressure.

  • Solid — particles tightly packed in fixed positions; definite shape & volume
  • Liquid — particles close but can slide; definite volume, no fixed shape
  • Gas — particles far apart moving fast; no fixed shape or volume
🔑 Phase ChangesMelting → Boiling → Sublimation (add heat) | Condensation → Freezing → Deposition (remove heat)
💡

Light — Reflection & Shadow

Physics · Class 6

Light Travels in Straight Lines

Light is a form of energy that travels in straight lines called rays at 3×10⁸ m/s.

  • Reflection — bouncing of light off a surface
  • Refraction — bending of light as it enters a different medium
  • Shadow — dark region formed when an opaque object blocks light
  • Umbra — complete shadow; Penumbra — partial shadow
🔑 Law of ReflectionAngle of Incidence = Angle of Reflection (both measured from the Normal)
🌍

The Solar System

Earth Science · Class 6

Our Solar System

The Solar System consists of the Sun and 8 planets orbiting it due to gravitational force.

  • Inner planets (rocky) — Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars
  • Outer planets (gas giants) — Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune
  • Asteroid Belt — between Mars and Jupiter
  • Earth's distance from Sun — 1 AU = 150 million km
🔑 Mnemonic"My Very Educated Mother Just Served Us Nachos" (Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune)

Fractions, Decimals & Percentages

Mathematics · Class 6

Understanding Fractions

A fraction represents a part of a whole. The top number is the numerator, the bottom is the denominator.

  • Proper fraction — numerator < denominator (e.g., 3/4)
  • Improper fraction — numerator > denominator (e.g., 7/4)
  • Mixed number — whole + fraction (e.g., 1¾)
  • Equivalent fractions — 1/2 = 2/4 = 3/6 = 50%
½ = 0.5 = 50% | ¼ = 0.25 = 25% | ¾ = 0.75 = 75%

Class 7 Biology, Chemistry, Physics, Earth Science & Math

🌱

Photosynthesis

Biology · Class 7

How Plants Make Food

Photosynthesis is the process by which plants use sunlight, carbon dioxide and water to produce glucose and oxygen.

  • Chlorophyll — green pigment in chloroplasts that captures sunlight
  • Light reactions — occur in thylakoid; split water, produce ATP
  • Dark reactions (Calvin Cycle) — occur in stroma; fix CO₂ into glucose
  • Stomata — tiny pores on leaves for gas exchange
6CO₂ + 6H₂O + Light Energy → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂
⚛️

Atoms & Molecules

Chemistry · Class 7

Building Blocks of Matter

An atom is the smallest particle of an element. Molecules are formed when two or more atoms are joined by chemical bonds.

  • Protons (+) — in nucleus; determine the element
  • Neutrons (neutral) — in nucleus; add mass
  • Electrons (-) — orbit nucleus in shells
  • Atomic Number — number of protons
  • Covalent bond — shared electrons; Ionic bond — transferred electrons
Atomic Mass = Protons + Neutrons | Atomic No. = Protons
🚀

Motion, Speed & Velocity

Physics · Class 7

Types of Motion

Motion is the change in position of an object with time relative to a reference point.

  • Distance — total path length (scalar)
  • Displacement — shortest path between two points (vector)
  • Speed — distance/time (scalar); Velocity = displacement/time (vector)
  • Acceleration — rate of change of velocity
Speed = Distance / Time | Acceleration = (v – u) / t
🪨

The Rock Cycle

Earth Science · Class 7

Rocks Change Continuously

  • Igneous — formed by cooling magma (granite, basalt)
  • Sedimentary — formed by layering of particles (sandstone, limestone)
  • Metamorphic — formed by heat & pressure on existing rocks (marble, slate)
  • Weathering — breaking down rocks by wind, water, ice
🔑 Key FactThe rock cycle has no beginning or end — rocks continuously transform from one type to another over millions of years.
🔢

Introduction to Algebra

Mathematics · Class 7

Algebra — Letters Represent Numbers

Algebra uses letters (variables) to represent unknown values and expresses relationships as equations.

  • Variable — a letter representing an unknown (x, y, n)
  • Expression — combination of terms (3x + 5)
  • Equation — expression with an equals sign (3x + 5 = 14)
  • Solving — finding the value that makes the equation true
If 2x + 3 = 11, then x = (11−3)/2 = 4

Class 8 Biology, Chemistry, Physics, Earth Science & Math

🫁

Human Digestive System

Biology · Class 8

Digestion: Breaking Down Food

The digestive system breaks down food into nutrients that the body can absorb into the bloodstream.

  • Mouth — mechanical & chemical digestion (saliva/amylase)
  • Stomach — HCl acid + pepsin breaks down proteins
  • Small intestine — main absorption of nutrients (6–7 m long!)
  • Liver — produces bile to emulsify fats
  • Large intestine — absorbs water; forms waste
🔑 Key FactIt takes 24–72 hours for food to pass through the entire digestive system.
📊

The Periodic Table

Chemistry · Class 8

Organizing the Elements

The Periodic Table arranges 118 elements by increasing atomic number in rows (periods) and groups (families with similar properties).

  • Period — horizontal row; elements in same period have same electron shells
  • Group — vertical column; elements share similar chemical properties
  • Metals — left side; conduct heat & electricity
  • Non-metals — right side; poor conductors
  • Noble gases — Group 18; very stable, rarely react
Valence electrons = Group number (for main groups)
⚡

Electric Circuits — Series & Parallel

Physics · Class 8

How Circuits Work

Electric current is the flow of electrons through a conductor. Circuits can be connected in series or parallel.

  • Series circuit — single path; same current through all components
  • Parallel circuit — multiple paths; same voltage across all branches
  • Ohm's Law — V = I × R (Voltage = Current × Resistance)
  • Short circuit — very low resistance path causing large current
Series: R_total = R₁ + R₂ | Parallel: 1/R = 1/R₁ + 1/R₂
🌤️

Earth's Atmosphere & Weather

Earth Science · Class 8

Earth's Protective Layers

  • Troposphere — contains 75% of all air; all weather happens here
  • Stratosphere — Ozone (O₃) layer absorbs harmful UV rays
  • Mesosphere — coldest layer; burns up most meteors
  • Thermosphere — extremely hot; auroras borealis occurs here
  • Greenhouse effect — CO₂, CH₄ trap heat warming Earth's surface
🔑 Key FactTemperature decreases as altitude increases in the troposphere (6.5°C per km).

Lines, Angles & Triangles

Mathematics · Class 8

Geometry — Shapes & Their Properties

  • Acute angle — less than 90°; Right angle — exactly 90°; Obtuse — 90°–180°
  • Complementary angles — sum = 90°; Supplementary — sum = 180°
  • Equilateral triangle — all sides equal (60°, 60°, 60°)
  • Isosceles — two equal sides; Scalene — no equal sides
Pythagoras: a² + b² = c² | Area of triangle = ½ × base × height

Class 9 Biology, Chemistry, Physics, Earth Science & Math

🧬

DNA Structure & Genetics

Biology · Class 9

The Blueprint of Life

DNA (Deoxyribonucleic Acid) carries the genetic information of all living organisms in the form of a double helix.

  • Nucleotides — building blocks: Adenine, Thymine, Guanine, Cytosine
  • Base pairing rule — A pairs with T; G pairs with C
  • Gene — a segment of DNA coding for a specific trait
  • Dominant (A) — expressed when present; Recessive (a) — hidden by dominant
  • Genotype — genetic makeup (AA, Aa, aa); Phenotype — physical appearance
🔑 Key FactGregor Mendel's experiments with pea plants in the 1860s established the laws of inheritance.
🔥

Chemical Reactions & Equations

Chemistry · Class 9

Reactants Transform into Products

A chemical reaction involves breaking bonds in reactants and forming new bonds in products, with energy exchange.

  • Exothermic — releases energy as heat (combustion, respiration)
  • Endothermic — absorbs energy from surroundings (photosynthesis)
  • Activation energy (Ea) — minimum energy needed to start a reaction
  • Catalyst — lowers activation energy; not consumed in reaction
  • Law of Conservation of Mass — atoms are neither created nor destroyed
CH₄ + 2O₂ → CO₂ + 2H₂O (combustion of methane)
🍎

Newton's Three Laws of Motion

Physics · Class 9

Newton's Laws of Motion (1687)

  • 1st Law (Inertia) — an object stays at rest or in uniform motion unless acted upon by an external force
  • 2nd Law — F = ma; larger force → greater acceleration; larger mass → smaller acceleration
  • 3rd Law — for every action there is an equal and opposite reaction (rocket propulsion, swimming)
F = ma | p = mv (momentum) | Impulse = F × t = Δp
📈

Quadratic Equations & Parabolas

Mathematics · Class 9

Equations with x² — Parabolas

A quadratic equation has a squared variable. Its graph is a U-shaped curve called a parabola.

  • Standard form — ax² + bx + c = 0
  • Vertex — the turning point (minimum if a>0, maximum if a<0)
  • Roots/Zeros — where the parabola crosses the x-axis
  • Discriminant (b²−4ac) — tells how many real solutions exist
Quadratic Formula: x = (−b ± √(b²−4ac)) / 2a
🌋

Plate Tectonics & Earthquakes

Earth Science · Class 9

Earth's Moving Plates

Earth's crust is broken into large pieces called tectonic plates that float on the semi-molten mantle.

  • Divergent boundary — plates move apart; new crust forms (Mid-Atlantic Ridge)
  • Convergent boundary — plates collide; mountains or ocean trenches form
  • Transform boundary — plates slide horizontally; causes earthquakes (San Andreas Fault)
  • Subduction — denser oceanic plate sinks under continental plate → volcanoes
🔑 Key FactThe Indian plate collides with the Eurasian plate at ~5 cm/year, forming the Himalayas!

Class 10 Biology, Chemistry, Physics, Earth Science & Math

Cell Division — Mitosis & Meiosis

Biology · Class 10

How Cells Divide

  • Mitosis — produces 2 identical diploid (2n) cells; for growth & repair
  • Meiosis — produces 4 genetically unique haploid (n) cells; for sexual reproduction
  • Stages of Mitosis — Prophase → Metaphase → Anaphase → Telophase → Cytokinesis
  • Crossing over — in Meiosis I; creates genetic variation
  • Chromosome number — humans have 46 (23 pairs) in diploid cells
🔑 Key FactCancer is caused by uncontrolled mitosis where cells divide without stopping.
🧪

Acids, Bases & the pH Scale

Chemistry · Class 10

Acids, Bases & Neutralisation

  • Acids — pH < 7; taste sour; turn blue litmus red; HCl, H₂SO₄, HNO₃
  • Bases — pH > 7; feel slippery; turn red litmus blue; NaOH, NH₃
  • pH 7 — neutral (pure water)
  • Indicators — litmus, phenolphthalein, methyl orange detect acid/base
  • Salt — product of acid-base neutralisation
pH = -log[H⁺] | Strong acid: completely ionizes; Weak acid: partial ionization
🌊

Waves — Properties & Sound

Physics · Class 10

Waves Transfer Energy

  • Transverse waves — particles vibrate ⊥ to wave direction (light, water)
  • Longitudinal waves — particles vibrate ∥ to wave direction (sound)
  • Amplitude — height from rest to crest; related to energy
  • Frequency (f) — waves per second (Hz); Wavelength (λ) — length of one complete wave
  • Electromagnetic spectrum — radio waves to gamma rays (all travel at c = 3×10⁸ m/s)
v = fλ | T = 1/f | Decibels: 0 dB (threshold) to 120 dB (painful)
📐

Trigonometry — SOH-CAH-TOA

Mathematics · Class 10

Ratios of Triangle Sides

Trigonometry studies the relationship between angles and sides of right-angled triangles.

  • SOH — Sin = Opposite / Hypotenuse
  • CAH — Cos = Adjacent / Hypotenuse
  • TOA — Tan = Opposite / Adjacent
  • Pythagorean identity — sin²θ + cos²θ = 1
  • Applications — heights of buildings, distances, navigation
sin²θ + cos²θ = 1 | tanθ = sinθ/cosθ

Class 11 Biology, Chemistry, Physics, Earth Science & Math

🧠

Nervous System & the Neuron

Biology · Class 11

The Body's Communication Network

The nervous system consists of the brain, spinal cord, and nerves — coordinating body responses through electrical impulses.

  • CNS — Central Nervous System (brain + spinal cord); processes information
  • PNS — Peripheral Nervous System; connects body to CNS
  • Sensory neurons — carry signals TO brain; Motor neurons — FROM brain to muscles
  • Myelin sheath — fatty insulation; speeds up nerve impulse (up to 120 m/s)
  • Reflex arc — automatic response bypassing the brain (e.g., knee-jerk)
Speed of nerve impulse: 1–120 m/s | Synapse gap: ~20 nm
🌡️

Thermodynamics & Entropy

Chemistry · Class 11

Energy, Heat & Disorder

  • 0th Law — if A=T(B) and B=T(C), then A is in thermal equilibrium with C
  • 1st Law — ΔU = Q − W; internal energy = heat added − work done
  • 2nd Law — entropy (disorder) of an isolated system always increases
  • Gibbs Free Energy — ΔG = ΔH − TΔS; negative ΔG = spontaneous reaction
  • Enthalpy (ΔH) — heat absorbed/released at constant pressure
ΔG = ΔH − TΔS | ΔS = q_rev/T | Cp = dH/dT
🧲

Electromagnetism & Maxwell's Equations

Physics · Class 11

Light is an Electromagnetic Wave

Electromagnetic waves are oscillating electric and magnetic fields perpendicular to each other and to the direction of propagation.

  • Maxwell's 4 equations — unify electricity, magnetism and light
  • Faraday's Law — changing magnetic field induces electric field (generators)
  • Ampère's Law — current creates magnetic field (electromagnets)
  • EM spectrum — radio (km) → gamma rays (pm); all travel at c in vacuum
  • Lenz's Law — induced current opposes the change causing it
F = qE + qv×B (Lorentz force) | c = 1/√(μ₀ε₀)

Calculus — Derivatives & Integrals

Mathematics · Class 11

The Mathematics of Change

Calculus deals with rates of change (derivatives) and accumulation of quantities (integrals).

  • Derivative f'(x) — instantaneous rate of change; slope of tangent line
  • Integral ∫f(x)dx — area under the curve; accumulation of change
  • Chain rule — d/dx[f(g(x))] = f'(g(x)) × g'(x)
  • Product rule — d/dx[f·g] = f'g + fg'
  • Fundamental Theorem of Calculus — differentiation and integration are inverse operations
d/dx(sin x) = cos x | d/dx(eˣ) = eˣ | d/dx(ln x) = 1/x

Class 12 Biology, Chemistry, Physics, Earth Science & Math

🌎

Ecosystem, Biomes & Carbon Cycle

Biology · Class 12

How Ecosystems Sustain Life

An ecosystem is a community of living organisms interacting with their physical environment through nutrient cycles and energy flows.

  • Carbon cycle — carbon moves between atmosphere, living things, soil and oceans
  • Greenhouse gases — CO₂, CH₄ trap heat; rising levels cause climate change
  • Nitrogen cycle — N₂ fixed by bacteria → ammonia → nitrates → plants → animals
  • Biomes — tropical rainforest, desert, tundra, grassland, taiga, ocean
  • Biodiversity — variety of species; indicator of ecosystem health
🔑 Key FactOceans absorb ~25% of CO₂ emitted by humans, acting as crucial carbon sinks.
🧴

Organic Chemistry — Carbon Compounds

Chemistry · Class 12

Chemistry of Carbon Compounds

Organic chemistry studies carbon-based compounds. Carbon's ability to form 4 bonds makes millions of compounds possible.

  • Homologous series — Alkanes (CₙH₂ₙ₊₂), Alkenes (CₙH₂ₙ), Alkynes (CₙH₂ₙ₋₂)
  • Isomers — same molecular formula, different structural arrangement
  • Addition reaction — alkenes react with H₂/halogens across C=C bond
  • Substitution — alkanes replace one atom; e.g., halogenation
  • Polymerisation — monomers join to form polymers (plastics, proteins)
Esterification: Alcohol + Acid → Ester + H₂O | Saponification: Ester + NaOH → Soap
☢️

Nuclear Physics — Fission & Fusion

Physics · Class 12

Energy from the Nucleus

  • Nuclear fission — heavy nucleus (U-235) splits into smaller nuclei + energy + neutrons
  • Chain reaction — neutrons from fission trigger more fissions (nuclear reactors, bombs)
  • Nuclear fusion — light nuclei (H) join to form heavier nucleus (He) + enormous energy
  • E = mc² — even tiny mass converts to huge energy (c² = 9×10¹⁶ m²/s²)
  • Half-life — time for half of radioactive atoms to decay
E = mc² | Activity A = λN | t½ = ln2/λ | Binding energy = mass defect × c²
🎲

Probability & Statistics

Mathematics · Class 12

Measuring Chance & Data

Probability quantifies the likelihood of events; statistics analyses and interprets data.

  • P(A) — probability of event A = favourable outcomes / total outcomes
  • Bayes' Theorem — P(A|B) = P(B|A)×P(A) / P(B); conditional probability
  • Normal distribution — 68% within 1σ; 95% within 2σ; 99.7% within 3σ
  • Mean, Median, Mode — measures of central tendency
  • Standard deviation (σ) — measures spread of data around mean
σ = √[Σ(x−μ)²/n] | P(A∩B) = P(A)×P(B) for independent events
🌡️

Climate Change & Global Warming

Earth Science · Class 12

Earth's Warming Climate

  • Greenhouse effect — natural process; GHGs trap outgoing IR radiation, warming Earth
  • Enhanced GH effect — human-added CO₂/CH₄ intensifies trapping → global warming
  • Consequences — melting glaciers, rising sea levels, extreme weather, species loss
  • Carbon footprint — total GHG emissions by an individual/organization
  • Mitigation — renewable energy, reforestation, carbon capture, energy efficiency
🔑 Paris Agreement GoalLimit global warming to 1.5°C above pre-industrial levels by reducing CO₂ emissions to net zero by 2050.

  • Gene — a segment of DNA coding for a specific trait
  • Dominant (A) — expressed when present; Recessive (a) — hidden by dominant
  • Genotype — genetic makeup (AA, Aa, aa); Phenotype — physical appearance
🔑 Key FactGregor Mendel's experiments with pea plants in the 1860s established the laws of inheritance.

Chemical Reactions & Equations

Chemistry · Class 9

Reactants Transform into Products

A chemical reaction involves breaking bonds in reactants and forming new bonds in products, with energy exchange.

  • Exothermic — releases energy as heat (combustion, respiration)
  • Endothermic — absorbs energy from surroundings (photosynthesis)
  • Activation energy (Ea) — minimum energy needed to start a reaction
  • Catalyst — lowers activation energy; not consumed in reaction
  • Law of Conservation of Mass — atoms are neither created nor destroyed
CH₄ + 2O₂ → CO₂ + 2H₂O (combustion of methane)

Newton's Three Laws of Motion

Physics · Class 9

Newton's Laws of Motion (1687)

  • 1st Law (Inertia) — an object stays at rest or in uniform motion unless acted upon by an external force
  • 2nd Law — F = ma; larger force → greater acceleration; larger mass → smaller acceleration
  • 3rd Law — for every action there is an equal and opposite reaction (rocket propulsion, swimming)
F = ma | p = mv (momentum) | Impulse = F × t = Δp

Quadratic Equations & Parabolas

Mathematics · Class 9

Equations with x² — Parabolas

A quadratic equation has a squared variable. Its graph is a U-shaped curve called a parabola.

  • Standard form — ax² + bx + c = 0
  • Vertex — the turning point (minimum if a>0, maximum if a<0)
  • Roots/Zeros — where the parabola crosses the x-axis
  • Discriminant (b²−4ac) — tells how many real solutions exist
Quadratic Formula: x = (−b ± √(b²−4ac)) / 2a

Plate Tectonics & Earthquakes

Earth Science · Class 9

Earth's Moving Plates

Earth's crust is broken into large pieces called tectonic plates that float on the semi-molten mantle.

  • Divergent boundary — plates move apart; new crust forms (Mid-Atlantic Ridge)
  • Convergent boundary — plates collide; mountains or ocean trenches form
  • Transform boundary — plates slide horizontally; causes earthquakes (San Andreas Fault)
  • Subduction — denser oceanic plate sinks under continental plate → volcanoes
🔑 Key FactThe Indian plate collides with the Eurasian plate at ~5 cm/year, forming the Himalayas!

Cell Division — Mitosis & Meiosis

How Cells Divide

Acids, Bases & Neutralisation

Thermodynamics & Entropy

Chemistry · Class 11

Energy, Heat & Disorder

  • 0th Law — if A=T(B) and B=T(C), then A is in thermal equilibrium with C
  • 1st Law — ΔU = Q − W; internal energy = heat added − work done

Electromagnetism & Maxwell's Equations

Physics · Class 11

Light is an Electromagnetic Wave

Electromagnetic waves are oscillating electric and magnetic fields perpendicular to each other and to the direction of propagation.

  • Faraday's Law — changing magnetic field induces electric field (generators)

Calculus — Derivatives & Integrals

Mathematics · Class 11

The Mathematics of Change

Calculus deals with rates of change (derivatives) and accumulation of quantities (integrals).

  • Derivative f'(x) — instantaneous rate of change; slope of tangent line
  • Integral ∫f(x)dx — area under the curve; accumulation of change
  • Chain rule — d/dx[f(g(x))] = f'(g(x)) × g'(x)
  • Product rule — d/dx[f·g] = f'g + fg'
  • Fundamental Theorem of Calculus — differentiation and integration are inverse operations
d/dx(sin x) = cos x | d/dx(eˣ) = eˣ | d/dx(ln x) = 1/x

Ecosystem, Biomes & Carbon Cycle

Biology · Class 12

How Ecosystems Sustain Life

An ecosystem is a community of living organisms interacting with their physical environment through nutrient cycles and energy flows.

  • Carbon cycle — carbon moves between atmosphere, living things, soil and oceans
  • Greenhouse gases — CO₂, CH₄ trap heat; rising levels cause climate change
  • Nitrogen cycle — N₂ fixed by bacteria → ammonia → nitrates → plants → animals
  • Biomes — tropical rainforest, desert, tundra, grassland, taiga, ocean
  • Biodiversity — variety of species; indicator of ecosystem health
🔑 Key FactOceans absorb ~25% of CO₂ emitted by humans, acting as crucial carbon sinks.

Organic Chemistry — Carbon Compounds

Chemistry · Class 12

Chemistry of Carbon Compounds

Organic chemistry studies carbon-based compounds. Carbon's ability to form 4 bonds makes millions of compounds possible.

  • Homologous series — Alkanes (CₙH₂ₙ₊₂), Alkenes (CₙH₂ₙ), Alkynes (CₙH₂ₙ₋₂)
  • Isomers — same molecular formula, different structural arrangement
  • Addition reaction — alkenes react with H₂/halogens across C=C bond
  • Substitution — alkanes replace one atom; e.g., halogenation
  • Polymerisation — monomers join to form polymers (plastics, proteins)
Esterification: Alcohol + Acid → Ester + H₂O | Saponification: Ester + NaOH → Soap

Energy from the Nucleus

  • Nuclear fission — heavy nucleus (U-235) splits into smaller nuclei + energy + neutrons
  • Chain reaction — neutrons from fission trigger more fissions (nuclear reactors, bombs)
  • Nuclear fusion — light nuclei (H) join to form heavier nucleus (He) + enormous energy
  • E = mc² — even tiny mass converts to huge energy (c² = 9×10¹⁶ m²/s²)

Probability & Statistics

Mathematics · Class 12

Measuring Chance & Data

Probability quantifies the likelihood of events; statistics analyses and interprets data.

  • P(A) — probability of event A = favourable outcomes / total outcomes
  • Bayes' Theorem — P(A|B) = P(B|A)×P(A) / P(B); conditional probability
  • Normal distribution — 68% within 1σ; 95% within 2σ; 99.7% within 3σ
  • Mean, Median, Mode — measures of central tendency
  • Standard deviation (σ) — measures spread of data around mean
σ = √[Σ(x−μ)²/n] | P(A∩B) = P(A)×P(B) for independent events

Climate Change & Global Warming

Earth Science · Class 12

Earth's Warming Climate

  • Greenhouse effect — natural process; GHGs trap outgoing IR radiation, warming Earth
  • Enhanced GH effect — human-added CO₂/CH₄ intensifies trapping → global warming
